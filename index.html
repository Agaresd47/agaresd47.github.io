About

Concrete work across research, engineering, and modeling

My recent work has focused on three main directions. First, I build medical imaging systems that handle difficult acquisition conditions, including arbitrary-view chest X-rays, partial labels, and low-dose inputs. Second, I work on generative and representation-learning methods for reconstruction and anatomical consistency. Third, I build LLM-based pipelines for financial text understanding and structured signal extraction.

I am most interested in problems where the data are imperfect but the output still needs to be reliable. That is why a lot of my work involves synthetic data generation, weak supervision, robust training, data infrastructure, and end-to-end evaluation.

Research

Research themes

Medical Imaging Under Imperfect Supervision

I design segmentation and reconstruction pipelines for settings with noisy labels, partial annotations, and strong distribution shift.

Generative Models for Reconstruction

I work on diffusion-style denoising and latent-space priors for low-dose X-ray reconstruction, with attention to motion robustness and anatomical fidelity.

LLMs for Noisy Structured Domains

I build data pipelines and reasoning systems that turn long financial disclosures and heterogeneous records into usable structured signals.
