About

Applied AI with a strong bias toward real data and real constraints

My research centers on robust machine learning in settings where data are noisy, labels are incomplete, or deployment constraints actually matter. I am especially interested in medical imaging, multimodal learning, synthetic data generation, and model adaptation under imperfect supervision.

In parallel with academic research, I have worked on quantitative research and data engineering problems involving financial filings, longitudinal social-science data, and large-scale processing pipelines. I enjoy moving between modeling, infrastructure, and end-to-end experimentation.
